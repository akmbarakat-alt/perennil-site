Origin

Egypt did not inspire
this brand. Egypt produced it.

Same river. Same clay. Same hands passing something forward.

Six thousand years ago, the first Egyptian hands pulled clay from the Nile and shaped it into vessels for daily life. Every civilization that followed — Pharaonic, Nubian, Greek, Roman, Islamic, Ottoman — continued the same act.

The Nubians burnished the clay with stone until it sealed itself — no glaze, just mastery. The Islamic period brought true glazing — the clay became art and waterproof simultaneously. The Ottoman period refined it further. Each generation added one layer to what the previous one built.

The story never stopped. It just waited for the next pair of hands.

PERENNIL is those hands. The founder is Egyptian — carrying the same DNA as every qed, every fakhkhārī, every sannā'ī who ever sat at a wheel in Fustat or Aswan or Qena.

This is not a revival of a dead craft. It is the continuation of a living one.

Launch Collection · No. 01

Kerma.

Nubian · 4000 BC — Today

These patterns were on Nubian walls before the pyramids were built. The same hands. The same Nile. The same clay.

Archaeologists excavating near Kerma found geometric patterns on Nubian walls 4,000 years old — and recognized them immediately, because they decorate Nubian houses built today. An unbroken visual language across forty centuries. Not decoration. Armor. Identity written on walls.

Triangle Band

The foundational Nubian form. Painted in repeating rhythm.

Crescent & Star

Protection. Life. The centerpiece of every bowl.

Palm Tree

The Nile's edge, drawn as geometry.

Fish & Bird

The Nile as provider. Painted inside the vessel.

Diamond Grid

Wide fields. Perfect repetition. Mathematics before its name.
